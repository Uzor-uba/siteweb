What you should know about the saxophone

This is a simple guide to learning about the saxophone, its family of instruments, famous players, and trusted brands.

  • History
  • Saxophone Family
  • Brands
  • Famous Saxophonists

History

The saxophone was invented by Belgian instrument maker Adolphe Sax in 1840. Sax wanted to create an instrument that combined the rich power of brass with the agility and expressiveness of woodwinds.

He first designed the saxophone for military and orchestral use, and he patented it in Paris in 1846. Early versions were adopted by military bands in France and Belgium because the instrument could project strongly while still playing smooth, chromatic lines.

Important milestones

  • 1840 – Adolphe Sax begins developing the saxophone family.
  • 1846 – Sax patents the saxophone and introduces it in Paris.
  • 1850s – French military bands adopt the saxophone widely.
  • 1920s – Jazz musicians in the United States embrace the saxophone as a lead instrument.
  • Modern era – The saxophone remains essential in jazz, classical, pop, and film music.

From its origins as a military band instrument to its place at the heart of jazz, the saxophone has become one of the most beloved and versatile instruments in music history.

Saxophone Family

From bright soprano tones to deep baritone power, the saxophone family includes instruments for every musical voice.

Soprano Saxophone

The soprano saxophone is the smallest and highest-pitched member of the family. It often sounds bright and lyrical, making it popular in jazz and classical solos.

Alto Saxophone

The alto saxophone is the most common saxophone today. It balances warmth and agility, and is widely used in jazz, pop, and school bands.

Tenor Saxophone

The tenor saxophone has a rich, warm tone. It is a favorite in jazz and rock because it can play both melodic solos and strong rhythmic lines.

Baritone Saxophone

The baritone saxophone produces a deep, resonant sound and is often used in big bands, jazz ensembles, and film music to provide a solid low voice.

Popular Brands

These manufacturers are known for building saxophones that musicians trust on stage and in the studio.

Selmer

Selmer is a historic French brand famous for professional saxophones used by many jazz legends.

Yamaha

Yamaha makes reliable saxophones for students and professionals, known for smooth playability and consistent quality.

Yanagisawa

Yanagisawa is a Japanese maker respected for its handcrafted, detail-oriented saxophones with a warm, focused tone.

Famous Saxophonists

These saxophone players helped define the sound of jazz, soul, and modern popular music.

Charlie Parker

Known as "Bird," Parker revolutionized jazz with his fast improvisation, complex harmonies, and bebop style.

John Coltrane

Coltrane became one of the most influential figures in jazz, blending spirituality and technical mastery on the tenor saxophone.

Sonny Rollins

Rollins is celebrated for his powerful sound, inventive solos, and long career spanning more than seven decades.

Lester Young

Lester Young helped define the cool jazz sound with his relaxed phrasing and lyrical tenor saxophone playing.

Cannonball Adderley

Adderley brought soulful energy to hard bop and became a major voice in the alto saxophone world.

Listen & Learn

Try these classic recordings to hear the unique voice of each player.

  • Charlie Parker: "Koko"
  • John Coltrane: "Giant Steps"
  • Sonny Rollins: "St. Thomas"
  • Lester Young: "Lester Leaps In"
  • Cannonball Adderley: "Mercy, Mercy, Mercy"

These recordings are great starting points for anyone learning about saxophone history and style.
